18 Bible Verses About Strength for Hard Times

There are seasons when you simply run out — of energy, of nerve, of the ability to push through one more day. These eighteen verses are for exactly those seasons. They don't tell you to try harder; they point you to a strength outside yourself, and remind you where it comes from.

The Bible has a striking view of strength: it rarely tells the weary person to dig deeper. Instead it points them upward — to a God who "gives power to the weak," who becomes "the strength of my heart" precisely when our own gives out. That's good news on the days you feel anything but strong. The verses below are grouped by what you may be facing, so you can go straight to the ones you need. Take one to heart rather than skimming all eighteen. (Verses are quoted from the World English Bible, a modern, public-domain translation.)

When you feel weak or worn out

Scripture meets exhaustion not with a pep talk but with a promise: your weakness is exactly where God's strength shows up.

"but those who wait for Yahweh will renew their strength. They will mount up with wings like eagles. They will run, and not be weary. They will walk, and not faint." — Isaiah 40:31
"He gives power to the weak. He increases the strength of him who has no might." — Isaiah 40:29
"My grace is sufficient for you, for my power is made perfect in weakness." — 2 Corinthians 12:9
"My flesh and my heart fails, but God is the strength of my heart and my portion forever." — Psalm 73:26

Notice the paradox at the heart of these verses: strength comes not from denying your weakness but from bringing it to God. "My power is made perfect in weakness" turns the whole idea on its head — the empty place is where his strength has room to work.

When you need to remember that God is your strength

Over and over, the Bible's writers don't say they have strength — they say God is their strength. That shift changes everything about where you look on a hard day.

"Yahweh is my strength and my shield. My heart has trusted in him, and I am helped." — Psalm 28:7
"Yahweh, the Lord, is my strength. He makes my feet like deer's feet, and enables me to go on my high places." — Habakkuk 3:19
"Yah is my strength and song. He has become my salvation." — Exodus 15:2
"Don't be grieved, for the joy of Yahweh is your strength." — Nehemiah 8:10
"Yahweh is my light and my salvation. Whom shall I fear? Yahweh is the strength of my life. Of whom shall I be afraid?" — Psalm 27:1

Nehemiah's line is worth sitting with: "the joy of the Lord is your strength." Not grim determination, but joy — a quiet gladness in who God is — is what steadies and holds us up when circumstances would knock us flat.

When you need courage to keep going

Sometimes strength simply means taking the next step when you'd rather stop. These verses call us to stand firm — always with the reason attached.

"Be strong and courageous. Don't be afraid. Don't be dismayed, for Yahweh your God is with you wherever you go." — Joshua 1:9
"Be strong and courageous… for Yahweh your God himself is who goes with you. He will not fail you nor forsake you." — Deuteronomy 31:6
"Watch! Stand firm in the faith! Be courageous! Be strong!" — 1 Corinthians 16:13
"Finally, be strong in the Lord, and in the strength of his might." — Ephesians 6:10
"Be strong, and let your heart take courage, all you who hope in Yahweh." — Psalm 31:24

Every "be strong" here rests on something outside yourself — "for the Lord your God is with you," "in the strength of his might," "all you who hope in Yahweh." The courage isn't self-generated bravado; it's confidence in Who goes with you.

When you need strength through Christ

The New Testament locates our strength in a person — Christ himself, at work in us.

"I can do all things through Christ, who strengthens me." — Philippians 4:13
"strengthened with all power, according to the might of his glory, for all endurance and perseverance with joy." — Colossians 1:11
"Don't be afraid, for I am with you… I will strengthen you. Yes, I will help you. Yes, I will uphold you with the right hand of my righteousness." — Isaiah 41:10
"For God didn't give us a spirit of fear, but of power, love, and self-control." — 2 Timothy 1:7

Philippians 4:13 is often quoted as if it promised we can achieve anything we set our minds to. In context it's quieter and stronger than that: Paul had learned to be content in plenty and in want, and it was Christ who gave him the strength to endure either. That's the strength on offer — not to win every time, but to stand through anything.

How to hold on to these verses

Strength verses help most when they're already in you before the hard day arrives. When your own resolve is thin, you want God's words within reach, not on a shelf. A few simple practices make that happen:

  • Memorize one or two. A short verse like Philippians 4:13 or Psalm 28:7, committed to memory, comes to mind exactly when you need it — in the meeting, the hospital, the long night.
  • Write them by hand. Copying a verse slowly presses it deeper into memory and steadies the mind in the doing.
  • Pray them back to God. Turn a verse into a prayer — "Lord, be the strength of my heart today" — and write your own weakness beside it. Looking back later, you'll see how he carried you.

Frequently asked questions

What is the best Bible verse for strength?

Isaiah 40:31 and Philippians 4:13 are the two most turned to. Isaiah promises that those who wait on the Lord "will renew their strength… run, and not be weary," while Philippians reminds us we can endure all things "through Christ, who strengthens me." Psalm 28:7 — "Yahweh is my strength and my shield" — is another favourite.

What does the Bible say about strength in hard times?

The Bible consistently points weary people away from self-effort and toward God as the source of strength — "He gives power to the weak" (Isaiah 40:29), and "My power is made perfect in weakness" (2 Corinthians 12:9). Our weakness is presented as the very place God's strength shows up.

Are there short Bible verses about strength to memorize?

Yes. Some of the shortest and most powerful are "I can do all things through Christ, who strengthens me" (Philippians 4:13), "the joy of Yahweh is your strength" (Nehemiah 8:10), and "be strong in the Lord, and in the strength of his might" (Ephesians 6:10).

What does "the joy of the Lord is your strength" mean?

From Nehemiah 8:10, it means that gladness in who God is — rather than grim willpower — is what sustains and steadies us. Joy rooted in God gives a resilience that circumstances can't easily take away.
