How to make a Bible verse handwriting worksheet

Copying Scripture by hand is one of the oldest ways to learn it — slow enough that the words sink in. This free tool turns any verse into a clean practice page for children, whether they're just learning their letters or ready to copy a whole verse on their own.

  • Enter a verse. Add a reference for the title (optional) and paste the verse text from the translation you use.
  • Choose tracing or copywork. Trace prints the verse in light grey so a child can trace right over it. Copy shows the verse once as a model, then gives blank ruled lines to copy onto.
  • Pick a size. Large lines for little hands, smaller lines as they grow.
  • Print it. A Name and Date header makes it ready for a class set.

Ideas for using these sheets

These worksheets are a gentle fit for Sunday school centres, children's church, midweek clubs, and homeschool copywork. Pair a weekly memory verse with a matching tracing sheet, or let older children copy a short passage as morning work. Because you choose the verse and the translation, the sheet always matches what your family or class is learning together.

Frequently asked questions

Is this worksheet maker free?

Yes — free, with no account and no email. Make and print as many sheets as you like.

Do you save the verses I enter?

No. Everything is generated in your browser. Nothing you type is uploaded or stored. To keep a sheet, print it or use your browser's "Save as PDF" option.

What's the difference between tracing and copywork?

Tracing prints the verse in light grey so a child traces directly over the letters — best for young children. Copywork shows the verse once as a model and then gives blank ruled lines for the child to write it themselves.

Can I use it for a whole class?

Yes. Keep the Name / Date header on, print one copy, then photocopy a class set — or print straight from the tool for each child.

Which Bible translation does it use?

Whichever you paste in. The sheet uses your wording exactly, so it matches the Bible your class or family reads.
